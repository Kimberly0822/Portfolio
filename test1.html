Color Theory

Color theory is the art and science of how color works together and how they can effect our emotions when viewed. While most might think choosing a color for a project is an arbitrary thing and the designers' choice it is also good practice to keep in mind the rules of color theory. Choosing colors just because they're your favorite doesn't always mean it'll translate to a cohesive and legiable site. To start I'll list some of the basic terms that are good to remember

Types of Color

Color Wheel

  • The color wheel is a very important tool in color theory that helps you see the relationships between colors

Primary

  • These are the basic colors and cannot be made by mixing
    • Ex: Red, Yellow, and Blue

Secondary

  • These colors are made by mixing together two of the primary colors
    • Ex: Purple, Green, Orange

Tertiary

  • These colors can be made by mixing a primary color with a secondary color
    • Ex: Blue-Green, Red-Orange, Blue-Purple

Quanternary

  • Quanternary colors are made when you mix two tertiary colors together
    • These colors are more complex so they aren't on the basic wheel up above but some examples are slate (purple and green), and citron (green and orange)

Cool

  • These colors often give a peaceful or soothing vibe when used in a project
    • Blues is what you often think of when you hear cool colors but some purples, greens, and pinks fit into the category as well
